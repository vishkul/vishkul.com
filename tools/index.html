Marketing tools

3 live

Sharp, single-purpose tools for the problems AI is creating in marketing right now. Each one does a single job well.

$ ./aeo-checker ● LIVE

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude are becoming the new front page. This scans any URL and scores how readable it is to those answer engines, then hands you a ranked list of fixes that actually move the needle.

→ 0-100 score, grade, and priority fixes across crawler access, structured data, content clarity, and technical signals.
Run a scan →
$ ./brand-voice ● LIVE

Everyone claims their voice is "friendly and professional." This reads a site's actual copy and measures it: rhythm, formality, who it speaks to, energy, and the words it leans on. You get a profile to hand a writer or an AI, not a vibe.

→ tone, sentence rhythm, perspective, signature vocabulary, do's and don'ts, plus a paste-ready prompt for full guidelines.
Read a voice →
$ ./cold-email ● LIVE

Paste a cold email and get an honest teardown: a reply-readiness score, exactly what's killing your reply rate, and a prompt to rewrite it. Runs entirely in your browser.

→ reply-readiness score, what's working, ranked reply-rate killers, plus a paste-ready rewrite prompt.
Tear one down →

Experiments

4 live

Things I built because I couldn't not. Less useful, more fun, still no AI under the hood, just algorithms doing something you would not expect.

$ ./brand-from-a-word ● LIVE

Type one word and watch an algorithm build a whole brand around it: a color palette, a font pairing, a logo mark, and a voice direction, generated live and explained. No AI, no stock library, pure procedural design.

→ logo lockup, five-color palette, type pairing, voice line, and the reasoning behind each. Re-roll it endlessly.
Generate one →
$ ./browser-x-ray ● LIVE

A privacy mirror: everything your browser quietly hands to every site you visit. Screen, GPU, installed fonts, battery, timezone, and more, with nothing ever leaving the page.

→ a live fingerprint plus every signal it is built from, and how to shrink it.
See yours →
$ ./reverse-prompt ● LIVE

Paste AI-written text and it reconstructs the likely prompt behind it: the task, the tone, the format, the constraints. It cannot recover the real prompt, but it gets uncannily close.

→ a reusable reconstructed prompt, the signals it read, and the AI tells it spotted.
Reverse one →
$ ./image-to-blocks ● LIVE

Drop in any image and it gets colour-matched, cell by cell, against a palette of real Minecraft blocks with verified colours. Exact block counts for flat builds like pixel art, or a matched material palette for building a 3D thing from a reference photo. No AI, no API, just colour maths against a hand-checked block list.

→ a block-by-block map, exact quantities and stack counts, close alternatives per block, and Creative or stage-gated Survival palettes.
